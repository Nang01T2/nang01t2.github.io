ZDNet

websitetechnologynews

ZDNet is a tech news site1 made up of dozens of independent blogs2, owned and operated by Red Ventures3 alongside TechRepublic4. The site was founded in 1991 by Ziff Davis5 as one of the first known efforts to distribute tech articles online. Originally called ZiffNet6, the site was only accessible under the servers of the CompuServe ISP7 as an expansion on an earlier digital bulletin board system called PCMagNet included in the PCMagazine8. PCMagNet itself was a rebranding in 1988 of the original bulletin board launched in 1985.

In 1995 Ziff consolidated all his electronic publications under the single brand ZDNet and launched the public version of the site. ZDNet was finally bought in 2000 by its rival CNET Networks9 for 1.6M,inturnCNETwouldbelaterboughtbyCBSCorporationin2008for1.6M, in turn CNET would be later bought by CBS Corporation in 2008 for 1.6M,inturnCNETwouldbelaterboughtbyCBSCorporationin2008for1.8B. After CBS Corporation merged with Viacom to form ViacomCBS10 in 2019 ZDNet and TechRepublic were sold to RedVentures in 2020.

The website home page has an undeniable corporate feeling to it, with its articles and advertisements focusing entirely on the big business side of IT. In the site you can find a galley of technology photographs11, a section of videos with brief IT explanations12, reviews on various tech gadgets13 and their vast selections of articles14. Since it is a news site you can also join their newsletters15. Finally, alongside with TechRepublic they host a considerable library of software16 and white papers17, a legacy from their former owner CNET.
