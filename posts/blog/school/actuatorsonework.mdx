## Magnetic Circuit Air Gap ##

**Exercise 1**

![Exercise Air Gap](/static/images/notes/ExerciseofMagnetiEquivalentCircuitAirGapActuators.png)

This is the magnetic circuit of a primitive relay. The coil has 500 turns and the core path length is 360mm. The air gap lengths are 1.5mm each. A flux density of 0.8T is required to actuate the relay. The core is made of cast steel. Calculate the current in the coil, the permeability and relative permeability of the core and the coil current needed to have 0.8T if the air gap was 0.

$$
\begin{gathered}
	F = (N)(i) = (H_c)(l_c) + (H_g)(l_g)(2)\\
	\text{From the graph: } 0.8 \, \text{T} =  510 \, \text{At/m}\\
	F_c = (H_c)(l_c) = (510)(0.36)\\
	F_c = 184 \, \text{At}\\
	B = (\mu)(H) \implies H_g = \frac{B_g}{\mu_0}\\
	F_g = (H_g)(l_g)(2) = \frac{(B_g)(l_g)(2)}{\mu_0}\\
	F_g = \frac{(0.8)(0.0015)(2)}{1.25663 \times 10^{-6}}\\
	F_g = 1909.8 \approx 1910 \, \text{At}\\
	(N)(i) = F_c + F_g \implies i = \frac{F_c + F_g}{N}\\
	i = \frac{184 + 1910}{500}\\
	\therefore i =  4.18\,\text{A}\\\\
	B_c = (\mu_c)(H_c) \implies \mu_c = \frac{B_c}{H_c} = \frac{0.8}{510}\\
	\therefore \mu_c = 0.00157\\\\
	\mu = (\mu_0)(\mu_r) \implies \mu_r = \frac{\mu_c}{\mu_0} = \frac{0.00157}{1.25663 \times 10^{-6}}\\
	\therefore \mu_r = 1250\\\\
	(N)(i) = (H_c)(l_c) \implies i = \frac{(H_c)(l_c)}{N} = \frac{(510)(0.36)}{500}\\
	\therefore i_2 = 0.36\,\text{A}
\end{gathered}
$$

**Exercise 2**

![Exercise Air Gap](/static/images/notes/ExerciseTwoMagnetiEquivalentCircuitAirGapActuators.png)

In this magnetic circuit the relative permeability of the core is 1200. Neglect magnetic leakage and fringing. All length measurements are in centimeters. The material has a square cross sectional area. Calculate the air gap flux, the ait gap flux density and the magnetic field strength inside the air gap.

$$

	\Re_{bafe} = \Re_{bcde} \, \text{ Symmetry}\\
	\Re_{be} = \frac{l_{be}}{(\mu_c)(A_c)} = \frac{(0.52)-(0.005)}{(0.00150796)(0.0004)}\\
	\Re_{be} = 0.82 \times 10^{6} \, \text{At/wb}\\

	5000 = (2.58 \times 10^{6})(\Phi_1) + (0.82 \times 10^{6} + 9.94 \times 10^{6})(\Phi_1 + \Phi_1) \\

	B_g = \frac{\Phi_g}{A_g} = \frac{4.14 \times 10^{-4}}{0.0004}\\
	\therefore B_g = 2.035 \, \text{T}\\\\
	H_g = \frac{B_g}{\mu_0} = \frac{2.035}{(1.25663 \times 10^{-6})}\\
	\therefore H_g = 0.82 \, \text{At/m}
\end{gathered}

## Energy Conversion ##

![Energy Relay Example](/static/images/notes/FieldEnergyStoredExerciceOneActuators.png)

The magnetic core is made from cast steel and we need to produce a 1T flux density in the air gap. Calculate the DC voltage needed and the final stored field energy in the magnetic circuit.

$$
\begin{gathered}
	N = 250 \land R = 5\Omega \land l_g = 5\,\text{mm}\\\\
	(N)(i) = (H_c)(l_c) + (H_g)(l_g)\\
	i = \frac{(670)(0.6)+(\frac{(1)(2)(0.005)}{1.2567 \times 10^{-6}})}{250}\\
	i = 33.44 \,\text{A}\\
	V = (R)(i) = (5)(33.44)\\
	\therefore V = 167.2 \, \text{V}\\\\
	W_f = (w_{fc})(V_c) + (w_{fg})(V_g)\\
	w_{fc} \approx \frac{(b)(h)}{2} = \frac{(670)(1)}{2} = 335 \, \text{J/m}^3\\
	\text{Integrating Linear Approximation}\\
	\mu_c = \frac{B_c}{H_c} = \frac{1}{670} = 0.0015\\
	w_{fc} \approx \frac{(1)^2}{2(0.0015)} - \frac{(0)^2}{2(0.0015)}\\
	w_{fc} = 333.33 \, \text{J/m}^3 \approx 335\, \text{J/m}^3\\
	V_c = (A_c)(l_c) = ((0.05)(0.1))(0.6) = 0.003\,\text{m}^3\\
	W_{fc} = (335)(0.003) = 1.005\,\text{J}\\\\
	w_{fg} \approx \frac{(1)^2}{2(1.2567 \times 10^{-6})} - \frac{(0)^2}{2(1.2567 \times 10^{-6})}\\
	w_{fg} = 397900 \, \text{J/m}^3\\
	V_g = (A_g)(l_g) = ((0.05)(0.1))(0.01) = 0.00005\,\text{m}^3\\
	W_{fg} = (397900)(0.00005) = 19.89\,\text{J}\\\\
	W_f = 1.005 + 19.89 = 20.9 \, \text{J}\\
	\therefore W_f = 20.9 \, \text{J}
\end{gathered}
$$

## Energy and CoEnergy ##

The $\lambda - i$ curve is given by the linkage - current expression $i = (\frac{\lambda g}{0.09})^2$ , valid for $0 < i < 4$ and $0.03 < g < 0.1$. Find the mechanical force of the moving part when the operating point is $i=3$ and $g=0.05$ using the energy and coenergy methods.

$$
\begin{gathered}
	dW_f' = \int_{0}^{i} \lambda \, di\\
	dW_f' = \int_{0}^{i} \frac{(0.09)(i^{1/2})}{g} \, di = \frac{(0.09)(i^{3/2})}{(g)\frac{3}{2}} \\
	dW_f' = \frac{(0.09)(2)(i^{3/2})}{3g}\\
	f_m = \frac{dW_f'(i,x)}{dx} = \frac{d}{dg}\left(\frac{(0.09)(2)(i^{3/2})}{3g}\right)\\
	f_m = -\frac{(0.09)(2)(i^{3/2})}{3g^2}\\
	f_m (i=3 , g=0.05) = -\frac{(0.09)(2)((3)^{3/2})}{3(0.05)^2}\\
	\therefore f_m = - 129.7\,\text{N}\\\\
	W_f = \int_{0}^{\lambda} (i) \, d\lambda = \int_{0}^{\lambda} \left(\frac{\lambda g}{0.09}\right)^2 \, d\lambda\\
	W_f = \frac{\lambda^3 g^2}{0.09^2 (3)}\\
	f_m = -\frac{dW_f'(\lambda , x)}{dx} = -\frac{d}{dg}\left(\frac{\lambda^3 g^2}{0.09^2 (3)}\right)\\
	\lambda = \frac{(0.09)\sqrt{3}}{0.05} = 3.118\\
	f_m = - \frac{(2)(g)(\lambda)^3}{(3)(0.09)^2} = - \frac{(2)(0.05)(3.118)^3}{(3)(0.09)^2}\\
	\therefore f_m = - 129.7\,\text{N}
\end{gathered}
$$

## Rotating Machines ##

**Exercise 1**

![Exercise of Torque in Rotating](/static/images/notes/RotatorExercisePositionInductanceActuators.png)

We are given the inductance function of a rotating machine as $L(\theta)=(25.4)+(8.3)\cos(2\theta) + (1.8)\sin(4\theta)$, with its values in cm. Find the torque expression for an applied stator current of 3.5 A and the rotor position (angle) that will produce the maximum (negative) torque.

$$
\begin{gathered}
	\text{Only Stator Coil Active}\\
	\frac{i_s^2}{2} \frac{d(L_{ss}(\theta))}{d\theta}
	T = \frac{i_s^2}{2} \frac{d}{d\theta} (0.0254+0.0083\cos(2\theta) + 0.0018\sin(4\theta))\\
	T = \frac{i_s^2}{2} ((-(2)(0.0083)\sin(2\theta)) + ((4)(0.0018)\cos(2\theta)))\\
	T = (3.5^2)((-0.0083\sin(2\theta)) + ((2)(0.0018)\cos(2\theta)))\\
	\therefore T = -0.1017\sin(2\theta) + 0.044\cos(4\theta) \, \text{Nm}\\\\
	\sin(2\theta) = 1 \implies \theta = 45°\\
	\cos(4\theta) = -1 \implies \theta = 45°\\
	T_{max} = -0.1017(1) + 0.044(-1) = -0.1457 \, \text{Nm}\\
	\therefore \theta_{max} = 45°
\end{gathered}
$$

**Exercise 2**

We are given the inductance function of a reluctance (no rotor coil) rotating machine as $L_{ss}(\theta)=(L_0)+(L_2)\cos(2\theta)$. Find the torque expression for a stator with an applied AC current of $i_s = I_{sm}\sin(\omega t)$. If $\theta = \omega_m t + \delta$ where $\omega_m$ is the rotor angular velocity at any time $t$ and $\delta$ is the rotor position at $t=0$, calculate the  verage torque expression and the conditions where we have a nonzero average torque.

$$
\begin{gathered}
	T = \frac{i_s^2}{2} \frac{dL_{ss}(\theta)}{d\theta}\\
	T = \frac{((I_{sm}\sin(\omega t))^2)}{2} \frac{d}{d\theta} ((L_0)+(L_2)\cos(2\theta))\\
	T = \frac{(I_{sm}^2\sin^2(\omega t))}{2} (-(2)(L_2)\sin(2\theta))\\
	T = -(I_{sm}^2)(\sin^2(\omega t))(L_2)(\sin(2\theta))\\
	T = -(I_{sm}^2)(\sin^2(\omega t))(L_2)(\sin(2(\omega_m t + \delta)))\\
	T = -(I_{sm}^2)(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{\cos(2\omega t)}{2})(L_2)(\sin(2(\omega_m t + \delta)))\\
	T = -\frac{(I_{sm}^2)(L_2)}{2} [\sin(2\omega_m t + 2\delta) - (\sin(2\omega_m t + 2\delta))(\cos(2\omega t))]\\
	\sin(A)\cos(B) = \frac{1}{2}(\sin(A+B)+\sin(A-B))\\
	A = 2\omega_m t + 2\delta \land B = 2\omega t \land C = \omega_m t + \delta \land D = \omega t\\
	T = -\frac{(I_{sm}^2)(L_2)}{2} [ (\sin(A)) \\ 
	\;\;- ((\frac{1}{2})(\sin(2(C + D)))) \\
	\;\;- ((\frac{1}{2})(\sin(2(C - D)))) ]\\
	\therefore T = -\frac{(I_{sm}^2)(L_2)}{2} [ (\sin(2\omega_m t + 2\delta))\\
	\;\;- (\frac{1}{2})(\sin(2((\omega_m + \omega)(t) + \delta))) \\
	\;\;- (\frac{1}{2})(\sin(2((\omega_m - \omega)(t) + \delta))) ]\\
	\omega_m = 0 \implies T_{avg} = -\frac{(I_{sm}^2)(L_2)(\sin(2\delta))}{2}\\
	\omega_m + \omega = 0 \implies T_{avg} = \frac{(I_{sm}^2)(L_2)(\sin(2\delta))}{4}\\
	\omega_m - \omega = 0 \implies T_{avg} = \frac{(I_{sm}^2)(L_2)(\sin(2\delta))}{4}
\end{gathered}
$$

Note that the torque expression consists of three sinusoidal equations, if the coefficients that depend on time equal any value other than zero then the overall average torque will be zero, meaning that in order to have a nonzero average torque this coefficients that depend on time must equal zero.

**Exercise 1**

![Toroid Problem](/static/images/notes/FirstExamToroidProblemoActuators.png)

Given that the number of turns in the coil is $N = 200$, the internal radii is $r_i = 0.06$, the external radii is $r_e = 0.1$ and the core cross sectional permeability is $\mu_r = 1500$ calculate:

1. The mean length of the toroid.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		r_p = \frac{0.1+0.06}{2} = 0.08 \, \text{m}\\
		l_c = (2)(\pi)(r_p) = (2)(0.08)(\pi) = 0.16 \pi \, \text{m}
	\end{gathered}
	$$

2. The permeability of the core material.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		\mu_c = (\mu_r)(\mu_0) = (1500)(1.2566 \times 10^{-6}) = 0.001885
	\end{gathered}
	$$

3. The current required to produce a flux density of 1.8T at the mean radius.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		(N)(i) = (H_c)(l_c) = \frac{B_c}{\mu_c}(l_c)\\
		i = \frac{(B_c)(l_c)}{(\mu_c)(N)} = \frac{(1.8)(0.16\pi)}{(0.001885)(200)} = 2.399\,\text{A}
	\end{gathered}
	$$

4. The cross sectional area.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		d = 0.1-0.06 = 0.04 \, \text{m}\\
		A = \frac{\pi(d)^2}{4} = \frac{\pi(0.04)^2}{4} = 0.001256 \, \text{m}^2
	\end{gathered}
	$$

5. The core magnetic flux.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		\Phi = (B)(A) = (1.8 \, \text{T})(0.001256 \, \text{m}^2) = 0.00226 \, \text{Wb}
	\end{gathered}
	$$

6. The new current required to maintain the same flux density with a $0.0035$ length air gap. $27.449 \, \text{A}$

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		(N)(i) = (H_c)(l_c) + (H_g)(l_g) = \frac{B_c}{\mu_c}(l_c) + \frac{B_g}{\mu_g}(l_g)\\
		B_c = B_g \implies i = \frac{(B)(l_c + (l_g \mu_r))}{(\mu_r)(\mu_0)(N)}\\
		i = \frac{(1.8)((0.16\pi - 0.0035) + ((0.0035)(1500)))}{(1500)(1.2566 \times 10^{-6})(200)} = 27.449\,\text{A}
	\end{gathered}
	$$

**Exercise 2**

![Reluctance Motor Problem](/static/images/notes/ReluctanceMotorProblemActuators.png)

A reluctance motor with four rotor poles has a reluctance $\Re$ that varies with the position of the rotor with the function $\Re(\theta) = 200000 - 100000\cos(4\theta)$. The stator coil has $N = 200$ and a negligible resistance while connected to an AC supply of $120V_{rms}$ and $60Hz$.

1. The magnetic flux as a function of time.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		e = (N)(\frac{d\Phi}{dt}) = (A)(\sin(\omega t)) = 120\sqrt{2}\sin(\omega t)\\
		\Phi = \int \frac{e}{N} \, dt = \frac{1}{N} \int 120\sqrt{2}\sin(\omega t)\\
		u = \omega t \land \frac{du}{dt} = \omega = (2)(\pi)(f)\\
		\Phi = -\frac{120\sqrt{2}\cos(\omega t)}{(200)(\omega)} = -\frac{120\sqrt{2}\cos((120\pi) t)}{(200)(120\pi)}\\
		\Phi = -0.00225 (\cos(120\pi t))
	\end{gathered}
	$$

2. The torque as an expression of the reluctance and the magnetic flux.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		T = -\frac{(i_s^2)}{2} \frac{dL(\theta)}{d\theta}\\
		L = \frac{N^2}{\Re} \land F = (\Phi)(\Re) = (N)(i) \implies i = \frac{(\Phi)(\Re)}{N}\\
		T = -\frac{(\Phi)^2(\Re)^2}{(2)(N)^2} \left(\frac{(dL)(d\Re)}{(d\Re)(d\theta)}\right) = -\frac{(\Phi)^2(\Re)^2}{(2)(N)^2} -\left(\frac{N^2(d\Re)}{(\Re)^2(d\theta)}\right)\\
		T = -\frac{(\Phi)(d\Re(\theta))}{(2)(d\theta)}
	\end{gathered}
	$$

3. The specific torque expression of the system.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		\Re(\theta) = 200000 - 100000\cos(4\theta)\\
		\Phi = (\Phi_m) \cos(\omega t) = -0.00225 (\cos(\omega t))\\
		\frac{d\Re(\theta)}{d\theta} = 400000 \sin(4\theta) = (R)\sin(4((\omega_m t) + \delta))\\
		T = -\frac{(\Phi_m)^2(\cos^2(\omega t)) ((R)\sin(4((\omega_m t) + \delta)))}{(2)}\\
		\sin(A)\cos(B) = \frac{1}{2}(\sin(A+B)+\sin(A-B))\\
		R = 400000 \land \Phi_m^2 = -0.00225\\
		T = (0.506)( (\sin(4(\omega_m t + \delta))) +\\
		((0.5)(\sin((4\omega_m + 2\omega)(t) + 4\delta))) + \\
		((0.5)(\sin((4\omega_m - 2\omega)(t) + 4\delta))) )
	\end{gathered}
	$$

4. The speed values of the rotor where it has a nonzero average torque.

	$$
	\begin{gathered}
		\omega_m = 0\\
		(4)(\omega_m) + (2)(120(\pi)) = 0 \implies \omega_m = -60\pi\\
		(4)(\omega_m) - (2)(120(\pi)) = 0 \implies \omega_m = -60\pi
	\end{gathered}
	$$

5. Maximum possible torques when the rotor is at its nonzero average torque speed values.

	$$

2. Calculate the efficiency of the motor.

$$
\begin{gathered}
	\eta = \frac{P_{out}}{P_{in}} = \frac{P_{mech} - P_{rot}}{P_{in}}\\
	P_{mech} = I_2' \frac{R_2' (1-s)}{s}\\
	I_2' = \frac{j X_m I_1}{jX_m + jX_2' + \frac{R_2'}{s}}\\
	I_2' = \frac{j 10 (71.42 \angle -34.3)}{j 10 + j 0.25 + \frac{0.1}{0.138}}\\
	I_2' = \frac{(10 \angle 90)(71.42 \angle -34.3)}{10.28 \angle 85.97}\\
	I_2' = \frac{714.2 \angle 55.7}{10.28 \angle 85.98} = 69.47 \angle -30.2\\
	P_{mech} = (3)(69.47)^2 (0.721)(1-0.138) = 8998.2\\
	\eta = \frac{8998.2 - 250}{12262.52} = 0.71\\
	\therefore \eta = 71\%
\end{gathered} 
$$

3. Calculate the maximum torque.

$$
\begin{gathered}

\end{gathered}
