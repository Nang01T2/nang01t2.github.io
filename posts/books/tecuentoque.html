TCQ or T Cuento Q was a micro-story contest that took place in Uruguay thanks to the work of the radio broadcast La Maquina de Pensar, and phone carrier ANTEL. The contest was made known in Montevideo by asking its citizens to send a short story via SMS with a limit of 160 characters. In the span of 5 years nearly 150,000 stories were collected by the radio station publishing annually their best 100 through their multiple blogs La Maquina de Pensar Blog, Sopa de Letras TCQ Blog and Sopa Desvan TCQ Blog.

The micro story is a magical artifact, filled to the brim with ingenuity, where its development is merely one more ornament of its climax. It is also a space where we are allowed to anthropomorphize animals and common objects, attributing them with unsuspected purposes. Death is generally the main character thanks to its similarities with micro stories themselves, they are both brief, unexpected, decisive and mysterious.

Suicides, murders, ghosts, disappearances and monsters frequently populate the micro story universe. The brevity of the medium induces their authors to fill in the gaps by referencing other famous literary works like Don Quixote, religious texts like the Bible or simply imagining alternative historical circumstances to the ones we know. Strangely there is also an abundance of micro stories themed around fishermen, mermaids and many other creatures of the sea,

The micro story can be a summary, a tribute to the literary achievements of other authors such as Mario Benedetti and Horacio Quiroga. This expression is nothing more than a simple container into which we pour our current cultural ideologies, their authors manage to distill the life of their potential stories into just a few essential details, the micro story is as ephemeral as the digital society that cultivates them.
