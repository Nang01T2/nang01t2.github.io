Reference systems

The spherical coordinates are given by the orderly triad (Rho, Phi, Theta) or (Rho, Theta, Phi). Where (Rho) is the distance to the origin, (Theta) is the angle between the projection of (Rho) in XY and the axis X positive, (PHI) is the angle between (Rho) and (Z). Here (Z) is equal to (Rho) for the cosine of (PHI).

The plane Cartesian or Euclidean is the set of points (x, y) where both are real numbers. It is represented by two orthogonal lines that intersect at the origin.

  • The X is the ABCISA to the point. The Y is the orderly to the point.

Euclidean space is the set of points (X, Y, Z) where the three components or variables are real. It is represented by three orthogonal straights. If any component is 0 the space becomes a plane indicated by two axes.

  • (0, B, c) = plane YZ
  • (A, 0, c) = plane xz
  • (A, B, 0) = Flat XY

Cylindrical coordinates are denoted by (R, Theta, Z). Where (R) is the distance to the origin over XY, (Z) is the height and (theta) is the angle from the positive x axis.
